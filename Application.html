DUKE RR

Details about the upcoming KTM 1390 Super Duke RR have been leaked through homologation documents filed in Europe. We now know that the bike will be lighter by 12kg compared to the standard R variant, and could feature a lot more premium components.

The KTM 1390 Super Duke RR will be a more capable variant in the 1390 Super Duke lineup, and will be lighter than the R variant by 8.5kg. For perspective, the R variant weighs 212.5kg fully fueled. To achieve weight reduction, the brand has likely used the same set of upgrades that were applied to the KTM 1290 Super Duke RR.

Harley-Davidson

  • Power output remains unchanged
  • Lighter than the R variant by 8kg
  • Will only be available in limited numbers

Details about the upcoming Harley-Davidson Performance Edition have surfaced through internal product disclosures and regulatory filings. The motorcycle is expected to be lighter than the standard model, while retaining the same power output, focusing instead on handling, agility, and premium components.

This Harley-Davidson performance-focused variant will sit at the top of the lineup, aimed at riders looking for a more aggressive and refined riding experience. The standard version weighs approximately 225 kg fully fueled, and the weight reduction has been achieved through the use of lightweight body panels, forged alloy components, and premium braking hardware.

To accomplish this weight saving, Harley-Davidson is expected to follow a similar strategy used in its CVO and performance-oriented models, incorporating higher-grade materials, upgraded suspension, and exclusive styling elements. Due to its limited production nature, this model will be targeted at enthusiasts seeking exclusivity and enhanced on-road performance.
